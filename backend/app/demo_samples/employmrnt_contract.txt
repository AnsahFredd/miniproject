# EMPLOYMENT AGREEMENT

**BETWEEN:** TechVision Solutions Inc.  
**AND:** Sarah Michelle Johnson

## EMPLOYMENT AGREEMENT

This Employment Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into on January 15, 2024, between **TechVision Solutions Inc.**, a corporation incorporated under the laws of Delaware, with its principal place of business at 1250 Technology Drive, Suite 400, San Francisco, California 94103 ("Company"), and **Sarah Michelle Johnson**, an individual residing at 789 Maple Street, Palo Alto, California 94301 ("Employee").

## RECITALS

WHEREAS, Company desires to employ Employee in the capacity of Senior Software Engineer; and

WHEREAS, Employee desires to accept such employment subject to the terms and conditions set forth herein;

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants and agreements contained herein, and for other good and valuable consideration, the receipt and sufficiency of which are hereby acknowledged, the parties agree as follows:

## 1. EMPLOYMENT AND DUTIES

### 1.1 Position and Responsibilities
Company hereby employs Employee, and Employee hereby accepts employment with Company, as a Senior Software Engineer. Employee shall report directly to the Chief Technology Officer and shall perform such duties and responsibilities as are customarily associated with such position, including but not limited to:

a) Designing, developing, and maintaining software applications and systems;
b) Collaborating with cross-functional teams to define, design, and ship new features;
c) Ensuring the performance, quality, and responsiveness of applications;
d) Identifying and correcting bottlenecks and fixing bugs;
e) Maintaining code quality, organization, and automatization;
f) Participating in code reviews and technical documentation;
g) Mentoring junior developers and contributing to team knowledge sharing.

### 1.2 Best Efforts
Employee agrees to devote Employee's full business time, attention, and energies to the performance of Employee's duties hereunder and to perform such duties faithfully, industriously, and to the best of Employee's ability.

### 1.3 Location
Employee's primary work location shall be at Company's offices located at 1250 Technology Drive, Suite 400, San Francisco, California 94103. Company may, in its sole discretion, permit Employee to work remotely up to three (3) days per week, subject to Company's remote work policies.

## 2. TERM OF EMPLOYMENT

### 2.1 Commencement Date
Employee's employment hereunder shall commence on February 1, 2024 ("Commencement Date").

### 2.2 At-Will Employment
Employee's employment with Company is for no specified period and constitutes "at-will" employment. Employee acknowledges that this employment relationship may be terminated at any time, with or without cause, and with or without notice, by either Employee or Company.

## 3. COMPENSATION AND BENEFITS

### 3.1 Base Salary
As compensation for services rendered hereunder, Company shall pay Employee a base salary of One Hundred Fifty Thousand Dollars ($150,000) per annum, payable in accordance with Company's standard payroll practices, but no less frequently than monthly. Such salary shall be subject to applicable federal, state, and local tax withholdings.

### 3.2 Performance Bonus
Employee shall be eligible for an annual performance bonus of up to twenty percent (20%) of Employee's base salary, based on achievement of individual and company performance objectives to be established by mutual agreement between Employee and Company. Any bonus payment shall be made no later than March 31st of the year following the year in which it was earned.

### 3.3 Equity Compensation
Subject to approval by Company's Board of Directors, Employee shall receive stock options to purchase twenty-five thousand (25,000) shares of Company's common stock at an exercise price equal to the fair market value of such stock on the grant date. Such options shall vest over four (4) years, with twenty-five percent (25%) vesting on the first anniversary of the Commencement Date and the remaining seventy-five percent (75%) vesting monthly thereafter.

### 3.4 Benefits
Employee shall be entitled to participate in all employee benefit plans and programs generally available to Company's employees, subject to the terms and conditions of such plans. Current benefits include:

a) Health, dental, and vision insurance (Company pays 100% of premiums for Employee and 80% for dependents);
b) 401(k) retirement plan with Company matching up to 6% of base salary;
c) Twenty-one (21) days of paid vacation annually, increasing to twenty-six (26) days after three (3) years of service;
d) Twelve (12) days of paid sick leave annually;
e) Twelve (12) paid holidays per year;
f) Professional development allowance of $3,000 annually;
g) Flexible spending account for healthcare and dependent care expenses.

## 4. CONFIDENTIALITY AND PROPRIETARY INFORMATION

### 4.1 Confidential Information
Employee acknowledges that during the course of employment, Employee will have access to and become acquainted with various trade secrets, confidential information, and proprietary information of Company, including but not limited to:

a) Technical data, research, inventions, processes, and formulas;
b) Customer lists, supplier information, and business relationships;
c) Financial information, including costs, profits, sales, and pricing strategies;
d) Marketing plans, business strategies, and expansion plans;
e) Personnel information and compensation structures;
f) Software code, algorithms, and system architectures.

### 4.2 Non-Disclosure
Employee agrees that Employee will not, during the term of this Agreement or at any time thereafter, disclose to any person, firm, corporation, or other entity, or use for Employee's own benefit or the benefit of others, any Confidential Information obtained during the course of employment, except as required in the performance of Employee's duties hereunder or as required by law.

### 4.3 Return of Materials
Upon termination of employment for any reason, Employee shall immediately return to Company all documents, materials, and other property belonging to Company, including all copies thereof.

## 5. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

### 5.1 Work for Hire
Employee acknowledges that all work performed and all inventions, discoveries, improvements, and innovations made by Employee during the course of employment, whether alone or in conjunction with others, that relate to Company's business or result from Employee's access to Confidential Information, shall be deemed "work for hire" and shall be the sole and exclusive property of Company.

### 5.2 Assignment of Rights
To the extent any such work or inventions are not deemed "work for hire," Employee hereby assigns and transfers to Company all right, title, and interest therein, including all intellectual property rights.

### 5.3 Assistance
Employee agrees to assist Company in obtaining and maintaining patents, copyrights, and other intellectual property rights for such work and inventions, including executing all documents reasonably requested by Company.

## 6. NON-COMPETITION AND NON-SOLICITATION

### 6.1 Non-Competition
During the term of employment and for a period of twelve (12) months following termination of employment for any reason, Employee agrees not to directly or indirectly engage in, own, manage, operate, control, or participate in any business that competes with Company's business within the geographic areas where Company conducts business.

### 6.2 Non-Solicitation of Customers
During the term of employment and for a period of eighteen (18) months following termination, Employee shall not directly or indirectly solicit or attempt to solicit any customer or client of Company for the purpose of providing services or products competitive with those provided by Company.

### 6.3 Non-Solicitation of Employees
During the term of employment and for a period of twelve (12) months following termination, Employee shall not directly or indirectly solicit, recruit, or induce any employee of Company to terminate their employment with Company or to accept employment with any competitor of Company.

### 6.4 Reasonableness
Employee acknowledges that the restrictions contained in this Section 6 are reasonable and necessary to protect Company's legitimate business interests and that any violation would cause irreparable harm to Company.

## 7. TERMINATION

### 7.1 Termination by Company for Cause
Company may terminate Employee's employment immediately for "Cause," which shall include:

a) Material breach of this Agreement;
b) Conviction of a felony or crime involving moral turpitude;
c) Willful misconduct or gross negligence in the performance of duties;
d) Violation of Company policies or procedures;
e) Unauthorized disclosure of Confidential Information;
f) Failure to perform duties satisfactorily after written warning and reasonable opportunity to cure.

### 7.2 Termination Without Cause
Company may terminate Employee's employment at any time without Cause upon thirty (30) days' written notice. In such event, Employee shall receive severance pay equal to three (3) months of base salary, payable in accordance with Company's standard payroll practices.

### 7.3 Resignation
Employee may terminate employment at any time upon thirty (30) days' written notice to Company.

### 7.4 Effect of Termination
Upon termination for any reason, Employee shall be entitled only to compensation earned through the date of termination, plus any accrued but unused vacation time.

## 8. GENERAL PROVISIONS

### 8.1 Entire Agreement
This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the parties and supersedes all prior negotiations, representations, or agreements relating to the subject matter hereof.

### 8.2 Amendment
This Agreement may be amended only by written instrument signed by both parties.

### 8.3 Governing Law
This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of California, without regard to conflict of laws principles.

### 8.4 Jurisdiction
Any dispute arising under this Agreement shall be subject to the exclusive jurisdiction of the state and federal courts located in San Francisco County, California.

### 8.5 Severability
If any provision of this Agreement is held to be invalid or unenforceable, the remainder of this Agreement shall remain in full force and effect.

### 8.6 Waiver
No waiver of any provision of this Agreement shall be deemed or shall constitute a waiver of any other provision.

### 8.7 Assignment
This Agreement shall be binding upon and inure to the benefit of the parties and their respective successors and assigns, provided that Employee may not assign this Agreement without Company's prior written consent.

### 8.8 Notices
All notices required hereunder shall be in writing and delivered personally, by certified mail return receipt requested, or by recognized overnight courier service to the addresses set forth above.
